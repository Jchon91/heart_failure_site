Treatment of Heart Failure

The treatment of heart failure comes in two ways, which are medication and surgery. Prevention of Heart Failure through changes in lifestyle choices is also another way to treat heart failure, which wil be covered in the "Tips for a healthier lifestyle" section.

Medications

All of the medication options mentioned here cannot be found in stores and have to be perscribed by your doctor. Some medications doctors might use are:

  • ACE inhibitors (Angiotensin-convertying enzyme)This is a drug to lower blood pressure and improve blood flow, which decreases the workload on the heart.
  • Beta Blockers. These drugs help you slow down your heart rate if you are suffering from Heart arrhythmias and reduces blood pressure.
  • Diuretics. These help you prevent fluid buildup in your body by making you urinate more.
  • Digoxin. A drug that also slows down your heart rate.

Surgery

Another option to treat heart failure is surgery. Some surgeries that doctors use include:

  • Coronary bypass surgery. This is a surgery that opens up blocked arteries to allow blood to flow through your heart
  • ICD (implantable cardioverter-defibrillator) This is a device that is implanted under the skin in your chest with wires leading up to the veins and the heart. This "corrects" abnormal heart rates by giving a small shock to put your heart back into the correct rhythm.
  • Heart Transplant. This procedure basically replaces your heart with a heart from a donor.

You can greatly improve your heart or even prevent heart failure by making changes into your lifestyle. Head over to "Tips for a healthier lifestyle" section for more info.

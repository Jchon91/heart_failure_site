What is Heart Failure?

Simply put, heart failure, also known as congestive heart failure, means the heart is not pumping as well as it should be. Since the heart cannot pump enough blood efficiently, the blood backups the heart and causes fluids to build up in the lungs and the legs, causing the legs to turn swollen and blue.

Causes of Heart Failure

Heart failure can come from a variety of causes. Common causes of heart failure include:

  • High blood pressure
  • Heart muscle damage
  • Myocarditis (inflammation of the heart muscle)
  • Heart defects one was born with
  • Heart arrhythmias (heart beats too fast which weakens the heart overtime)
  • Sleep apnea (having trouble sleeping at night)
  • Smoking
  • Diabetes
  • Drinking too much alcohol

Warning signs of Heart Failure

There are there signs that can indicate heart failure. The three signs include:

  • Shortness of breath. The fluid buildup in the lungs from heart failure prevents oxygenated blood from pumping through the heart.
  • Fatigue or tiredness. The prevention of oxygenated blood can leave a person restless throughout the night Swelling of body parts. The fluid buildup in the lungs can lead to the swelling of other areas in your body such as your feet as explained earlier.
  • Coughing up pink colored mucus.

Although heart failure is very serious, with the right treatment, you can make your heart stronger and help you live longer. Head over to "Treatment of Heart Failure" below "About Heart Failure" to see treatment options.
